5 Principles Every Restaurant Owner Should Live By

For operators who want control, better margins, and long-term growth.

1

Never blindly hand control of your business to a third party—especially one that decides how much of your revenue it keeps.

Allowing powerful, expensive third-party apps to control your business is a fundamentally flawed way to operate. As things exist now, they determine how customers find you, how your menu is shown, and how much you earn from each order.

You must be the steward of your own ship, and good stewards embrace options that preserve operational control.

2

Better value and higher margins drive success—not higher prices.

Better value and healthier margins create repeat demand more reliably than raising prices to absorb high commissions.

Raising prices to offset high commissions reduces demand. Customers order less, switch options, or stop ordering altogether.

Studies show that even modest price increases lead to measurable drops in restaurant demand—for example, a 5% increase in price can reduce demand by roughly 7–10%.[1]

It is not a solution to hope customers will continue covering these unsustainable costs.

3

You don’t need to conquer the world. Conquer your local community.

Local traction matters. Strong restaurants win close to home by serving nearby customers who come back again and again.

Consistent business comes from nearby customers who order repeatedly. Broad exposure means little if it doesn’t convert into local, repeat demand.

4

Your menu is your first—and often only chance to make a good impression.

If your menu is unclear, hard to navigate, or incomplete, customers hesitate—or move on.

Clear, structured menus help customers decide faster and order with confidence.

5

Satisfied customers are your greatest promotional ally.

The most reliable growth comes from repeat customers and word-of-mouth. When customers consistently get good value and a smooth experience, they return—and they share their experience with others.

The Problem with the Current System

The current system is dominated by powerful third-party platforms that control how customers find restaurants, how menus are presented, and how revenue is distributed. While doing this, they restrict access to customer data—preventing restaurants from ever communicating directly or building lasting customer relationships.

Third-party platforms charge restaurants up to 30% per order. On top of that, customers absorb fees and markups of up to 15%. Add it up: as much as 45 cents of every dollar in a transaction is being extracted—from both sides simultaneously.

And to top it off, they decide when you get paid—holding onto and investing your hard-earned revenue before transmitting it to you.

45% of the pie is not just greedy—it’s abusive.

What restaurants lose

  • Up to 30% commission on every order
  • Margin that could go to better food, staff, or growth
  • The ability to build customer relationships and promote their own deals directly to their customers
  • Control over when they receive money they have already earned

How this impacts customers

  • Pay up to 45% more before service fees, taxes, tips, and delivery fees
  • Forced to seek alternatives, including dining out less frequently or cooking at home
  • Net effect: customers pay far more—and receive far less in value.
