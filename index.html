45% of the Pie is Just Greedy.

Here’s what the current model actually costs. Third-party platforms charge restaurants up to 30% per order. On top of that, customers absorb fees and markups of up to 15%. Add it up: as much as 45 cents of every dollar in a transaction is being extracted — from both sides simultaneously.

What restaurants lose

  • Up to 30% commission on every order
  • Control over their customer relationships
  • Margin that could go to better food, staff, or growth
  • The ability to promote their own deals directly

What customers pay for it

  • Service fees, delivery fees, and inflated menu prices
  • Up to 15% added to what the restaurant actually charges
  • Hidden markups baked in before they ever check out
  • Less value from every dollar they spend on food
That 45% isn’t going to better food, faster service, or lower prices. It’s being extracted from both sides of a transaction that neither side agreed to subsidize.
